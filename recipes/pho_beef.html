Vietnamese Beef Pho

This beef pho features a comforting, richly-seasoned meat broth ladled over rice noodles and thinly sliced sirloin.
Serve with hoisin, Sriracha, green onion, cilantro, bean sprouts, basil, and lime.
A take on southern Vietnamese pho, this is a comforting bowl of rich, spiced beef broth ladled over rice noodles and thinly sliced beef.

Ingredients

  • 5 pounds beef soup bones
  • 1 tablespoon salt, divided
  • Aromatics: Ginger, onion (or shallot)
  • 2 gallons water
  • medium onions, quartered
  • 1 (4 inch) piece fresh ginger root
  • 2 pounds beef oxtail
  • 1 white (daikon) radish, sliced
  • 2 ounces whole star anise pods
  • ½ (3 inch) cinnamon stick
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 2 whole cloves
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • salt to taste
  • 1 ½ pounds dried flat rice noodles
  • ½ pound frozen beef sirloin

Directions

  1. Step 1: Place beef bones in a 9-quart (or larger) pot; season with 1 teaspoon salt. Pour water into the pot and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer broth for about 2 hours.
  2. Step 2: Meanwhile, set an oven rack about 6 inches from the heat source and preheat the oven's broiler. Line a 10x15-inch roasting pan with aluminum foil.
  3. Step 3: Place onions and unpeeled ginger onto the prepared roasting pan and cook under the preheated broiler, stirring occasionally, until vegetables are charred, 10 to 15 minutes.
  4. Step 4: Cool slightly. Chop onions, then peel and slice ginger; set aside separately.
  5. Step 5: Skim fat from surface of simmering broth. Add oxtail, radish, and charred onions to broth. Tie charred ginger, anise, cinnamon stick, peppercorns, and cloves in cheesecloth to make a bouquet garni; add to broth. Stir in sugar, fish sauce, and remaining 2 teaspoons salt. Simmer over medium-low heat for at least 4 hours (the longer, the better). Season with salt.
  6. Step 6: Strain broth. Discard bones and bouquet garni. Reserve meat from bones for another use. Chill broth in the refrigerator, 8 hours to overnight.
  7. Step 7: Skim and discard fat from the top of chilled broth. Pour broth into a pot; bring to a boil. Reduce heat and keep hot until ready to serve.
  8. Step 8: Bring a pot of water to a boil. Turn off heat. Stir in rice noodles and let sit until noodles are tender yet chewy, 6 to 10 minutes.
  9. Step 9: Meanwhile, cut frozen sirloin into paper-thin slices.
  10. Step 10: Drain and divide noodles among bowls, about 1 1/2 cups per serving. Top each with a few sirloin slices. Ladle hot broth over sirloin and noodles.
  11. Step 11: Serve and enjoy!
